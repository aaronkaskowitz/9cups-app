Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 3, 2026

🔒 Your data never leaves your device

Overview

9 Cups ("the App") is a daily nutrition tracker built for people following therapeutic diets for MS and autoimmune conditions. We believe your health data is deeply personal and should stay entirely under your control.

The short version: We collect nothing. We store nothing on servers. We have no analytics, no tracking, no ads, and no accounts. Everything stays on your iPhone.

Data Collection

9 Cups does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on external servers. Specifically:

  • No account required. No email, no name, no phone number, no sign-up of any kind.
  • No analytics or tracking. We do not use Google Analytics, Firebase, Mixpanel, or any third-party analytics service.
  • No advertising. There are no ads and no ad-tracking SDKs in the App.
  • No network requests. The App does not connect to the internet. It functions entirely offline.
  • No health data sharing. The App does not integrate with Apple HealthKit, Google Fit, or any health data platform.

Data Storage

All data you enter (daily food logs, settings, display name) is stored locally on your device using Apple's CoreData framework. This data:

  • Lives only on your iPhone
  • Is protected by your device's passcode, Face ID, or Touch ID
  • Is included in your iCloud backups only if you have iCloud Backup enabled in your device settings (this is controlled by Apple, not by us)
  • Is permanently deleted if you uninstall the App

Third-Party Services

9 Cups uses zero third-party services, SDKs, or frameworks beyond Apple's standard iOS frameworks (SwiftUI, CoreData). There are no third-party libraries in the App.

Children's Privacy

9 Cups is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children. Since we collect no information from anyone, this is inherently satisfied.

Changes to This Policy

If we ever change this policy, we will update this page and the effective date above. Since we collect no data, meaningful changes are unlikely. If a future version introduces any network features, we will update this policy before release and make the changes clear.
